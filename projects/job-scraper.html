LinkedIn Job Scraper

Python Selenium BeautifulSoup Pandas Tkinter

Automated job postings collection from LinkedIn using Selenium, with salary extraction, pagination handling, and CSV export for trend analysis.

Problem

Manually browsing LinkedIn job listings is time-consuming and makes it hard to compare compensation across roles. There's no built-in way to export and analyze job data at scale for market research or personal job search optimization.

Approach

  • GUI Input: Tkinter dialog prompts for the job title to search
  • Browser Automation: Selenium with Firefox WebDriver handles LinkedIn login, search, and filtering (past week)
  • Pagination: Automatically navigates through multiple pages of results
  • Data Extraction: BeautifulSoup parses job title, company, location, and salary from listing HTML
  • Salary Normalization: Custom regex logic converts hourly, monthly, and annual salary ranges into standardized annual figures
  • Export: Results saved to CSV as {job_title}_{date}_export.csv

Technical Details

  • Salary parser handles multiple formats: $10/hr, $5000/month, $80K/yr — all normalized to annual ranges
  • Returns [lower_bound, upper_bound, average] for each posting
  • Helper functions validate DOM elements with null-safe checks before extraction
  • webdriver-manager auto-manages browser driver versions

Impact

  • Collects hundreds of job postings in minutes vs. hours of manual browsing
  • Structured CSV output enables pivot tables and trend analysis in Excel or Pandas
  • Salary normalization makes compensation comparison consistent across posting formats
