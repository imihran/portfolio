Customer Purchase Journey Analytics (Trade Up)

PySpark Spark SQL Pipeline Orchestration BI Dashboards Data Modeling

Designed a customer purchase journey analytics platform measuring base-to-premium product progression, with a novel customer classification methodology and an 8-stage data pipeline serving 7 product categories.

Problem

No way to quantify how customers progress from base-tier to premium products over time. Category teams needed empirical evidence of "Trade Up" behavior to inform vendor collaboration and customer engagement strategy, but the problem space was ambiguous with multiple valid approaches.

Approach

Developed a novel Trade Up customer classification methodology using T12M rolling windows with temporal ordering constraints (premium purchase must occur AFTER base purchase). Built an 8-stage pipeline with full dependency chain, Spark tuning, and a fallback mechanism for handling row overflow at scale.

Technical Details

  • Trade Up classification: T12M rolling window with temporal ordering constraint
  • Trade Up Retain metric: identifies customers sustaining premium purchasing behavior
  • 8-stage pipeline with explicit dependency chain (sequential + parallel fan-in stages)
  • Weighted SUMPRODUCT rollup to avoid double-counting overlapping customers at brand/vendor/category levels
  • LISTAGG fallback mechanism for row overflow at scale (15K+ recommendation items per base)
  • SQL dialect migration (warehouse-to-Spark) across all 8 stages with systematic function mapping
  • Pipeline optimization: inlined 5 intermediate tables as temp views to reduce stage count
  • Cross-project architecture leverage: reuses centralized attribute foundation table

Impact

  • Director-sponsored initiative directly informing customer engagement strategy
  • Enables category teams to quantify Trade Up behavior with empirical evidence
  • Supports vendor collaboration to identify "new money" opportunities
  • Featured in monthly business review presentation
  • Architecture pattern adopted as reference for future analytics pipelines
