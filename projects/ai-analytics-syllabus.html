AI in Analytics — Course Syllabus

Curriculum Design AI/LLM Prompt Engineering Analytics RAG Teaching

A 4-week, 8-session instructor-led course designed to make already-skilled analysts dramatically faster by treating AI as a co-analyst inside their existing workflow. Authored as a draft syllabus and pedagogical vision for an instructor role at the Armenian Code Academy (ACA).

Problem

AI is rewriting the role of the data professional. The analysts who win aren't the ones who resist these tools or the ones who blindly trust them — they're the ones who know exactly where to delegate to AI, where to verify it, and where to stay hands-on. The challenge: teach that judgment to working professionals in a single intensive month, without it becoming an intro-to-ML course or a tour of trendy apps.

Approach

A workflow-first curriculum built on three pedagogical principles:

  • Workflow-first, not tool-first: AI techniques are slotted into the analyst's real pipeline (acquire → clean → explore → model → communicate → govern), so the judgment outlives any specific tool.
  • Every session ships something: each class ends with a hands-on exercise on a realistic task, building a reusable personal toolkit of prompts, scripts, and patterns.
  • Trust, but verify: hallucination, reproducibility, privacy, and result validation are treated as core skills — participants learn to defend any AI-assisted output to a skeptical stakeholder.

The 8 Sessions

  • 1 — The AI-Augmented Analyst: landscape, mental models, and a "delegate to AI vs. do it yourself" decision framework.
  • 2 — Prompt Engineering for Analysts: reliable, reproducible, structured outputs and a reusable prompt library.
  • 3 — AI Pair-Programming for SQL & Python: generate, debug, refactor, and verify code before it touches production data.
  • 4 — Automating Data Preparation & Cleaning: profiling, fixing, and turning ad-hoc cleaning into repeatable scripts.
  • 5 — AI-Assisted EDA, Insight Generation & Reporting: from data to fact-checked, stakeholder-ready narrative.
  • 6 — Conversational Analytics: BI copilots, text-to-SQL, and an intro to RAG for querying internal docs and metrics.
  • 7 — Predictive & Advanced Analytics with AI Assistance: forecasting and classification with responsible interpretation.
  • 8 — Capstone, Governance & Ethics: an end-to-end AI-augmented workflow plus the professional guardrails.

Course Arc

  • Sessions 1–2 — Foundations: mental models + prompting (the bedrock skills)
  • Sessions 3–5 — Core acceleration: code, data prep, EDA & reporting (the daily wins)
  • Sessions 6–7 — Expanding scope: conversational + predictive analytics (the new reach)
  • Session 8 — Integration: capstone + governance (make it real and responsible)

Format

1 month · 8 sessions · 2 per week · 1.5–2 hours each · cohorts of 20–25 · live and hands-on. Tool-agnostic in principle but hands-on with current tools (ChatGPT/Claude with code interpreter, GitHub Copilot, a BI copilot, and a lightweight RAG setup). Adaptable for both B2C individual upskilling and tailored B2B corporate cohorts.
