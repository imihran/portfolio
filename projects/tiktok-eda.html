TikTok EDA & Hypothesis Testing

Python Pandas Seaborn SciPy Matplotlib

Exploratory data analysis and statistical hypothesis testing on TikTok claim verification data, investigating relationships between video metrics, claim status, author verification, and ban status.

Problem

TikTok needs to understand patterns in user-submitted claims to improve content moderation. The key question: do videos flagged as "claims" vs. "opinions" show statistically significant differences in engagement metrics like views, likes, shares, and comments?

Approach

  • Data Exploration: Analyzed a dataset of TikTok videos with claim status, verification status, ban status, and engagement metrics (views, likes, shares, downloads, comments)
  • Visualization: Distribution plots, box plots, and correlation heatmaps using Seaborn and Matplotlib to identify patterns
  • Statistical Testing: Hypothesis tests using SciPy to determine if engagement differences between claim types are statistically significant
  • Segmentation: Analyzed metrics across verified vs. unverified authors and active vs. banned accounts

Dataset

  • Video metadata: duration, transcription text
  • Claim status: claim vs. opinion
  • Author status: verified/not verified, active/under review/banned
  • Engagement: view count, like count, share count, download count, comment count

Impact

  • Identified statistically significant engagement patterns between claim types
  • Provided actionable insights for content moderation prioritization
  • Executive summary delivered for non-technical stakeholders
